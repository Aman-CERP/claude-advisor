# Community code of conduct

## Our standard

We want participation in Second Opinion by AmanERP to be safe, constructive, and useful. Contributors, maintainers, reviewers, and community members are expected to:

- communicate with respect and professional care;
- critique ideas and evidence rather than people;
- welcome different backgrounds, experience levels, identities, and viewpoints;
- protect private information and never request real credentials or confidential source for reproduction;
- disclose conflicts of interest and correct mistakes openly;
- prioritize user safety, accessibility, and the project's read-only trust boundary.

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized conduct, doxxing, deliberate intimidation, sustained disruption, and knowingly unsafe or deceptive technical guidance are not acceptable.

## Scope

This code applies in the repository, issue tracker, pull requests, reviews, release channels, and other project spaces. It also applies when someone represents the project in public.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to `hello@amanerp.com` with the subject “Second Opinion conduct report.” Do not use a public issue for a report that identifies a target or contains private information.

Maintainers will review reports in good faith, limit disclosure to people needed for resolution, and choose a proportionate response. Responses may include a private warning, content removal, temporary participation limits, or permanent exclusion from project spaces. Retaliation against a reporter or participant in an investigation is prohibited.

Project maintainers are responsible for applying this standard consistently and may remove contributions or comments that violate it.
